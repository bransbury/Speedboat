# Speedboat — Monthly Stakeholder Summary

[Home](../index.md) | [Docs](../docs/index.md) | [Guides](../guides/index.md) | [Ceremonies](../guides/ceremonies/index.md) | [Templates](index.md) | [Setup](../setup/index.md)

Prepare this every 4 weeks for leadership, Product, Sales, and adjacent stakeholders.

Use the Landing Log and the last 4 Weekly Snapshots as inputs. Keep it short. This is not a status report and not a roadmap deck.

The goal is to answer three questions:

1. What landed in the last 4 weeks?
2. What does the landing mix tell us?
3. What is likely coming next?

Draft it before the 4-week Learning Review so the team can look back at delivered outcomes and use that evidence in the discussion.

---

## Template

```
📍 Speedboat — Monthly Stakeholder Summary
   [Team name] — [Date range]

1. WHAT LANDED IN THE LAST 4 WEEKS?
   • [Outcome] — [Landing Type] — [one-line beneficiary/outcome]
   • [Outcome] — [Landing Type] — [one-line beneficiary/outcome]
   • [Outcome] — [Landing Type] — [one-line beneficiary/outcome]

2. WHAT DOES THE LANDING MIX TELL US?
   Customer: [N]
   Business: [N]
   Platform: [N]
   Decision: [N]

   [2-4 sentence readout.
   What pattern do we see?
   Is the mix healthy for this period?
   What trade-offs or learning matter most?]

3. WHAT IS LIKELY COMING NEXT?
   • [Likely next focus area or Landing]
   • [Likely next focus area or Landing]
   • [Known risk, dependency, or uncertainty if relevant]
```

---

## Tips

- **Derive, don't rewrite.** Pull from the Landing Log and Weekly Snapshots rather than creating a new reporting burden.
- **Lead with outcomes.** Stakeholders care more about what changed than about internal activity.
- **Show the mix, not just the highlights.** The balance between Customer, Business, Platform, and Decision Landings tells an important story.
- **Use "likely" on purpose.** This is not a commitment document. It should show direction and shaping, not false precision.
- **Keep it to one screen if possible.** If it gets long, you are adding more detail than stakeholders need.
