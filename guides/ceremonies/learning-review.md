# Every 4 Weeks — Learning Review

[Home](../../index.md) | [Docs](../../docs/index.md) | [Guides](../index.md) | [Ceremonies](index.md) | [Templates](../../templates/index.md) | [Setup](../../setup/index.md)

**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Facilitator:** Captain or rotating facilitator
**When:** Every 4 weeks
**Attendees:** Whole boat

---

## Purpose

Step back from the weekly flow and ask what the recent work is teaching the team.

This is not a blame session and not a generic "what was good or bad" retrospective. It is a lightweight learning loop for the operating model itself.

---

## Before the Meeting (Facilitator, 10 min prep)

- [ ] Review the last 4 weeks of the Landing Log
- [ ] Pull the last 4 Weekly Snapshots
- [ ] Note obvious patterns in WIP, Run load, and Landing mix
- [ ] When reviewing WIP, count meaningful work items, not individual tickets
- [ ] Bring 1–2 questions where the data suggests friction or drift

---

## Script

| Time | Activity | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 0–5 min | **Pattern review** | What do the last 4 weeks show about Landings, WIP, Preview decisions, and Run load? |
| 5–15 min | **What did we learn?** | What is the work teaching us about how this boat actually operates? Where did the model help? Where did it create friction? |
| 15–25 min | **Adjustments** | What 1–2 changes should we make for the next cycle? WIP limits, slicing, ceremony timing, role clarity, Preview discipline, or something else? |
| 25–30 min | **Commit** | Name the owner for each change and when you'll check whether it helped. |

---

## Good Questions

- "What is the Landing mix teaching us?"
- "Are our WIP limits helping us finish, or creating the wrong pressure?"
- "Are Previews generating clear decisions, or drifting?"
- "Is Run visible and sustainable?"
- "Are we learning fast enough without fragmenting attention?"
- "What should we try for the next 4 weeks?"

---

## Outputs

- [ ] 1–2 concrete changes for the next 4-week cycle
- [ ] An owner for each change
- [ ] A clear note of what evidence you'll watch next time

Keep it lightweight. If you leave with a long list, you are probably trying to change too much at once.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Don't turn it into a complaint session.
	Do anchor the conversation in the work and the evidence.
- Don't re-open every annoyance from the month.
	Do focus on the few patterns that matter most.
- Don't make five process changes at once.
	Do pick one or two adjustments and watch the effect.
- Don't judge people.
	Do examine the system, the flow, and the trade-offs.
- Don't skip it because Friday has a learning slot.
	Do treat weekly tactical learning and monthly model learning as different loops.
